Research

Multi-Component Bose-Einstein condensates

Matter made of matter waves! This new state of matter is achieved by cooling a gas of non-interacting bosonic particles below a critical temperature, Tc. As we cool down the atoms, the de-Broglie wavelength associated with the matter waves becomes longer and longer, and beyond Tc , the matter waves overlap such that all the particles oscillate in concert, forming a giant wave. This is known as Bose-Einstein condensation.

A BEC with spin internal degrees of freedom is referred to as the spinor BEC. Once the atoms with the non-zero hyperfine quantum number f are optically trapped, their spin degrees of freedom can vary due to the inter-atomic interactions. This spin diversity leads to a criterion of having 2f + 1 spin-variations over space and time.
